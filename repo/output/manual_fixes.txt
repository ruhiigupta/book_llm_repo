=== Introduction to Building Large Language Models | Large Language Models Basics ===
Large Language Models Basics

The core idea behind large language models is to create artificial intelligence systems that can process and understand human language at an unprecedented scale. This matters because it enables machines to generate coherent text, answer complex questions, and even converse with humans in a natural way. Technically, a large language model is a type of neural network designed to learn the patterns and structures of language by training on vast amounts of text data. This training process allows the model to develop a deep understanding of linguistic relationships, syntax, and semantics. For instance, a large language model can be trained on a dataset of news articles to learn the context and usage of words like "bank" as both a financial institution and the side of a river, enabling it to generate accurate and informative text on a wide range of topics.

=== Introduction to Building Large Language Models | Pretraining LLMs vs Finetuning LLMs ===
In building large language models, a crucial distinction exists between pretraining and finetuning. The core idea revolves around the model's exposure to data and its subsequent specialization. Pretraining involves training a model on a vast, diverse dataset to establish a broad foundation of language understanding. This stage enables the model to learn general patterns and relationships within language. In contrast, finetuning entails training a pre-trained model on a specific, often smaller dataset to adapt to a particular task or domain. For instance, a pre-trained model can be finetuned for sentiment analysis by training it on a dataset of labeled text examples, such as classifying movie reviews as positive or negative. This two-stage approach allows large language models to leverage the benefits of both general knowledge acquisition and specialized task adaptation.
